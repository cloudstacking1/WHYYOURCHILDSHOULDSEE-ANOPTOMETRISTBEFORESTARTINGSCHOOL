What is Dry Eye Syndrome?

Dry Eye Syndrome, or keratoconjunctivitis sicca, is a common condition that occurs when your tears aren't able to provide adequate lubrication for your eyes. This may happen for a variety of reasons your eyes might not produce enough tears, or the tears you do produce might evaporate too quickly. Either way, the result is discomfort, vision issues, and even damage to the surface of your eyes if left untreated.

Key Features

  • Artificial Tears and Lubricants:

    • For mild cases, over-the-counter artificial tears can provide temporary relief by lubricating the ocular surface. Lubricating gels or ointments may be recommended for overnight use to reduce symptoms during sleep.

  • Prescription Medications:

    • In cases where inflammation is present, medications such as cyclosporine A (Restasis) or lifitegrast (Xiidra) may be prescribed to reduce inflammation and increase tear production.

  • Punctal Plugs:

    • Punctal plugs are small devices inserted into the tear ducts to block drainage, thereby increasing tear retention on the ocular surface.?

  • Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) Therapy:

    • IPL therapy is an FDA-approved treatment that uses light pulses to reduce inflammation and improve meibomian gland function, addressing one of the most common causes of dry eye disease.
